# Metal-Semiconductor and MOS Junctions

## Summary

This chapter extends junction physics to metal-semiconductor interfaces, covering work function, electron affinity, and barrier height as the basis for Schottky barriers, Schottky diodes, and ohmic contacts. Students then study the MOS capacitor, tracing how gate voltage moves the semiconductor surface through accumulation, depletion, and strong/weak inversion, culminating in the threshold voltage that defines the on/off behavior of a MOSFET.
