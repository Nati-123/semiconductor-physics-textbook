# Intrinsic and Extrinsic Semiconductors

## Summary

This chapter introduces the distinction between intrinsic (pure) and extrinsic (doped) semiconductors, and surveys the elemental and compound semiconductor materials used in practice, including silicon, germanium, and gallium arsenide. Students learn how donor and acceptor atoms are introduced into the crystal lattice, setting up the doping concepts covered in the next chapter.
