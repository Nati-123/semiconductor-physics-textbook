# Doping, Ionization, and Temperature Regimes

## Summary

This chapter covers how donor and acceptor doping creates n-type and p-type material, and how dopant ionization energy determines when those dopants actually contribute free carriers. Students learn the freeze-out, extrinsic, and intrinsic temperature regions that describe how a doped semiconductor's behavior changes with temperature, along with compensated and degenerate doping.
