# Non-Equilibrium Carriers and Recombination

## Summary

This chapter moves beyond thermal equilibrium to describe excess carriers created by optical or thermal generation, and the recombination mechanisms — direct, indirect, trap-assisted (Shockley-Read-Hall), and Auger — that return a semiconductor to equilibrium. Students learn minority carrier lifetime, low- and high-level injection, and the continuity equation, arriving at the quasi-Fermi levels and steady-state carrier profiles used to analyze biased devices.
