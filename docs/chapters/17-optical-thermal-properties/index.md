# Optical and Thermal Properties of Semiconductors

## Summary

This chapter covers how semiconductors absorb light, from the absorption coefficient to photon absorption and photoconductivity, and connects this behavior to optoelectronic devices: photodiodes, solar cells, and light-emitting diodes. Students also learn radiative recombination and briefly consider thermal conductivity and thermal generation rate as complementary non-electrical properties of semiconductor materials.
